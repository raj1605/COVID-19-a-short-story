California : A COVID-19 Case Study




COVID-19 is a contagious disease that has now spread worldwide, leading to an ongoing pandemic.
Through the medium of these visualizations, we aspire to portray the importance of vaccines and also provide substantial information which enabled for the speedy delivery of vaccines to combat the disease.

California is the state with the highest population in the United States

(Hover over the states to know more)


California reported its first COVID case on 12th Feb, 2020

The first vaccine dose was administered on 14th Dec, 2020

There was less than one year between the first COVID case and the first vaccine

But, Do vaccines really make a change?

The decrease in COVID cases can be directly attributed to the vaccines as the infection rate drops
despite rigorous testing and no government imposed mandates

The world nations spent a total of 7.6 Billion Dollars for the COVID vaccine out of which 2.7 Billion Dollars were spent on mRNA vaccines.

The combined resources and collaborative efforts of different nations made it possible to develop the vaccine within a year


Multi-Threading in vaccine testing???

If the COVID vaccine was deployed in a traditional way, it would have taken at least 4 years for the administration of vaccine.
But, in this case all the 3 testing phases where launched in parallel,
resulting in rapid vaccine deployment within 1 year.

Vaccine Approval


Accelerated testing of the COVID Vaccine lead to the deployment of the vaccine in record time

Conclusion

Despite speculations regarding the efficacy of the vaccine, it is an undisputed fact that vaccines helped to slow down the infection rate.

We were able to advance the development and distribution of vaccines due to the prior knowledge we had about mRNA/coronaviruses, collective effort of the world nations and due to parallel testing.

Takeaways

It is important to get vaccinated against Covid-19 to end the deadly pandemic.

It is time for the world nations to set aside their differences and come together to combat deadly diseases.
